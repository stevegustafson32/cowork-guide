How It Works

Feed data flows in. Post ideas flow out.

LinkedIn Content Engine builds on top of your Feed Tracker data. It reads 7 days of network activity, finds content gaps, generates post ideas, and writes 3 variations of each idea with different hooks—all in your authentic voice.

The pipeline

Here's how data flows through the system:

Feed Tracker (Plugin 1)
↓ Collects 7 days of network posts
Content Engine (Plugin 2)
↓ Analyzes trends, finds gaps, generates ideas
↓ Creates 3 variations per idea (story/question/contrarian)
↓ Scores variations against your voice
Your Drafts (ready to post)
↓ You copy to LinkedIn manually
↓ Your engagement gets tracked
Performance Loop
↓ Next /generate biases toward what worked

Idea generation

Ideas are grounded in real data from your network:

  • Topic clustering — Finds the topics your network is talking about
  • Content gap analysis — Spots where your network is active but you're quiet
  • Supporting data — Shows you # of posts, average engagement, which connections authored them
  • Hook recommendation — Suggests which hook type works best for each topic

Example idea:

TOPIC: Remote Team Dynamics
WHY: 4 posts in your network this week. You last posted 3 weeks ago.
DATA: Avg engagement 34 likes, 9 comments
HOOK: Story (personal narrative works best for this topic)

Variations

3 hook types per idea. All written in your voice. All ready to post as-is:

Story Hook

"Just realized something watching my remote team scale... [personal moment] [lesson] [question at end]"

Question Hook

"Here's something I see a lot: great managers who struggle as leaders. Why is that? [reasoning]"

Contrarian Hook

"Unpopular opinion: you can't develop leaders by teaching management. You have to unlearn it first."

Scoring

Each variation gets scored based on:

  • Voice match — How well it captures your authentic tone
  • Hook strength — How compelling the opening is
  • Engagement potential — Based on your network's response to similar hooks
  • Audience fit — If Plugin 3 is installed, uses AI prediction of your audience response
Your voice, not generic LinkedIn

The engine learns your authentic voice from your recent posts. All variations sound like you, not like generic influencer-speak.

Learning loop

The system gets smarter with each post:

  1. You post a variationCopy from Content Engine to LinkedIn (manual).
  2. Feed Tracker tracks itCollects likes, comments, reposts on your posts.
  3. Content Engine reviewsMatches your actual posts to variations. Tracks performance.
  4. Pattern detectionUpdates hook_performance table. "Story hooks avg 67 likes. Listicles avg 12 likes."
  5. Next generation improvesIdeas and variations weighted toward high-performing hooks and topics.

Prerequisites

What you need to get started

Must-have: Feed Tracker (Plugin 1)

Content Engine reads from feeds.db—the database created and updated by Plugin 1. Without it, there's no data to analyze.

Required

Plugin 1 (LinkedIn Feed Tracker) must be installed, configured, and actively collecting data. If you haven't set it up yet, start there first.

Recommended: At least one week of data

Plugin 2's idea generation works best when Feed Tracker has collected posts from at least one week. This gives the engine enough signal to spot real trends and content gaps.

Optional: Plugin 3 (Audience Simulator)

If you install Plugin 3 later, Content Engine will automatically use its audience predictions to score variations. Without it, the engine self-scores based on your historical performance.

Cowork access

All commands run inside Cowork. You need a working Cowork installation and your regular Anthropic subscription.

Setup

Install and configure the engine

Step 1: Install the plugin

Install Content Engine from the Cowork Plugin Directory (or clone the repo manually from GitHub):

https://github.com/stevegustafson32/linkedin-content-engine-plugin

Step 2: Run setup

Open Cowork and run:

/setup-content-engine

What setup does (automatic)

  1. Checks Feed TrackerVerifies Plugin 1 is installed and has collected data.
  2. Analyzes your voiceReads your recent 20 posts and profiles your tone, length, emoji/hashtag usage.
  3. Sets authority topicsAsks you which topics you want to be known for (e.g., "Remote culture," "Leadership").
  4. Configures posting scheduleHow often you want to post (e.g., 3x/week at 9 AM and 2 PM).
  5. Initializes databaseCreates content_ideas, content_variations, and hook_performance tables in feeds.db.
  6. Generates first ideasCreates your first batch of post ideas (takes 2-3 minutes).
That's it

Setup takes 5-10 minutes. After that, you're ready to generate ideas and variations whenever you want.

Next: Generate your first ideas

Once setup is done, run:

/generate

Claude will show you 5-7 post ideas grounded in this week's network activity, with hooks suggested for each.

4
What You Get

Ideas, drafts, variations, and content calendar

Post Ideas (grounded in data)

Run /generate to get 5-7 ideas for this week's posts. Each idea shows:

  • The topic and why it's timely (network activity + your posting history)
  • Supporting data (# posts, avg engagement, top authors)
  • Suggested hook type for maximum resonance

Draft Posts (3 variations per idea)

Select an idea and Claude generates 3 variations with different hooks:

  • Story — Personal narrative + lesson (best for authenticity)
  • Question — Thought-provoking query (best for engagement)
  • Contrarian — Challenge conventional wisdom (best for controversy/reach)

All variations:

  • Are written in your authentic voice
  • Match your typical post length and style
  • Are ready to copy to LinkedIn and post as-is
  • Include emoji and hashtags if that's your style

Variation Scoring

Each variation gets scored on a scale (0-100) based on:

  • Voice match — How authentically it captures your tone
  • Hook strength — How compelling the opening is
  • Engagement potential — Based on your historical data
  • Audience fit — If Plugin 3 is active, includes audience prediction

Content Calendar

Run /calendar to see:

  • Upcoming ideas and when to post them
  • Which variations are scheduled
  • Your posting cadence over the next 2 weeks
  • Ability to reschedule, skip, or swap ideas

Performance Tracking

Run /review to see what's working:

  • Actual engagement on posted variations (likes, comments, reposts)
  • Hook performance breakdown ("Story hooks avg 67 likes")
  • Topic performance ("Leadership content gets 40% more engagement")
  • Trends in what resonates with your audience

Content Repurposing

Ask Claude: "Give me thread ideas from this week's posts" or "Create a carousel version of [topic]"

The engine can extend single posts into threads, turn posts into carousels, or create follow-up posts on high-performing topics.

You stay in control

All variations are suggestions. You always decide what to post, when to post, and whether to edit before posting. This keeps you in the creative driver's seat.

The Ecosystem

All three plugins working together

Content Engine is Plugin 2 in a 3-plugin LinkedIn ecosystem. Each plugin builds on the others:

Plugin 1: Feed Tracker

What it does: Collects every post from your network. Tracks your own post performance. Stores everything in a local database.

Why Content Engine needs it: All post ideas are grounded in real network trends. Without Feed Tracker data, Content Engine has nothing to analyze.

Learn more about Feed Tracker ↗

Plugin 2: Content Engine

What it does: Generates post ideas and variations from your network trends. Scores them against your voice. Learns from your performance.

What it reads: Posts from Plugin 1.

What it writes: Post ideas, variations, and performance learning loops.

Plugin 3: Audience Simulator

What it does: Predicts how your audience will respond to different post variations.

How it helps: Content Engine sends variation text to Plugin 3, gets back a prediction score (0-100), and shows you both scores so you can decide which variation to post.

Currently: In development. Check back soon.

The data flow

Plugin 1 collects → Your network posts, your posts, connections

Plugin 2 analyzes → Finds trends, gaps, generates ideas, writes variations

Plugin 3 predicts (optional) → Scores variations based on audience fit

You post → Copy variation to LinkedIn

Plugin 1 tracks → Real engagement on your posts

Plugin 2 learns → Updates performance loop, next generation improves
